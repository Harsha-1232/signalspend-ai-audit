# Economics

Assumption: Credex makes money when a company buys discounted AI infrastructure credits. If a qualified startup is spending $3,000/month on eligible AI usage and Credex helps them route $2,000/month through discounted credits, the annual credit volume is $24,000. If Credex earns a 10-15% gross margin on that resale or brokerage spread, one converted customer is worth roughly $2,400-$3,600 gross margin per year. I will model $3,000 annual gross margin per converted lead.

Channel CAC can be close to zero in cash but not in time. A founder-led X/HN/manual-audit channel might require 20 hours to produce 100 completed audits. Valuing time at $50/hour, that is $1,000 implied CAC for the cohort. If 100 audits create 35 emails, 8 consultations, and 2 purchases, CAC per customer is $500 and first-year gross margin is about $3,000, a 6x gross-margin payback.

The funnel that makes this profitable: audit completed to email capture at 30-40%, email capture to consultation at 15-25% for high-savings users, and consultation to purchase at 20-30%. On 1,000 audits, that means 350 leads, about 70 calls, and 14-21 purchases. At $3,000 annual gross margin each, that is $42,000-$63,000 first-year gross margin before labor.

For this tool to drive $1M ARR in 18 months, Credex needs either higher average contract value or large volume. At $3,000 gross margin/customer/year, it needs around 334 active converted customers. If the close rate from audit to purchase is 2%, the tool needs about 16,700 completed audits. That is possible only if the shareable report loop works, Credex publishes credible benchmark data, and high-savings users trust the recommendation enough to book without heavy sales effort.
