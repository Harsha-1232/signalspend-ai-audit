# GTM

The exact target user is a technical founder, VP Engineering, or founding engineer at a 5-50 person seed or Series A startup that has started paying for multiple AI tools without a procurement process. They are not yet large enough for a full finance team, but their Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, and API bills are now visible in the monthly burn review.

Right before they want this tool, they are likely searching or reading things like “Cursor team pricing too expensive,” “Claude Team vs Pro,” “GitHub Copilot Business worth it,” “OpenAI API bill high,” or posts about AI agents becoming surprisingly expensive. They also notice the pain in Slack when teammates ask for another paid seat or when the founder sees a Stripe receipt from a tool they forgot existed.

The first 100 users should come from specific founder/operator channels, not broad SEO. I would post teardown-style audits in Indie Hackers, r/SaaS, r/startups, r/LocalLLaMA cost threads, Hacker News “Show HN,” YC founder Slack if accessible, Latent Space Discord, AI Engineer Discord, and X replies under credible posts about AI tooling costs. The format would be specific: “Drop your stack and team size; I’ll reply with a 3-line AI spend audit.” Each useful reply links to SignalSpend for the full report.

With $0 budget, I would personally run 30 manual mini-audits in public, ask five founders to share anonymized before/after screenshots, and turn the strongest examples into a short launch thread. The unfair Credex channel is access to discounted-credit market knowledge: most generic calculators can say “downgrade,” but Credex can say when retail spend is large enough to move through credits.

Week-1 traction if it works: 300 unique visitors, 120 completed audits, 35 captured emails, 8 high-savings consultation clicks, and 3 qualified calls with startups spending more than $1,000/month on AI tools.
