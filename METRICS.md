# Metrics

The North Star metric is **qualified savings discovered per week**, defined as total monthly savings found for audits with at least $500/month potential savings and a captured email. This fits the product better than DAU because buyers use an audit tool episodically; the business value is not repeat usage, it is credible savings attached to reachable accounts.

The first input metric is audit completion rate: visitors who finish the form and generate a report. If this is low, the form is too long or the promise is unclear. The second is high-savings rate: completed audits with more than $500/month in potential savings. If this is low, distribution is reaching hobbyists rather than companies with meaningful AI spend. The third is consultation intent rate: high-savings users who click or submit the Credex follow-up path.

I would instrument page view, form started, tool added, audit generated, report shared, lead captured, high-savings callout viewed, consultation clicked, and email delivered. I would also track selected tools and use case so pricing-rule improvements can be prioritized around real stacks.

A pivot trigger: after 500 completed audits from relevant startup channels, if fewer than 5% show $500/month savings or fewer than 10% of high-savings users request follow-up, the positioning is wrong. Either the tool is attracting the wrong users, or the audit output is not trusted enough to move people toward Credex.
